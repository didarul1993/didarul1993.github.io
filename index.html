Didarul Islam, Ph.D.

Earth Observation, GeoAI, and Geospatial Data Science

I am an Earth observation scientist specializing in multi-sensor remote sensing, GeoAI, and scalable geospatial computing for environmental monitoring, climate impact assessment, wildfire risk, forest structure mapping, and coastal resilience.

View Projects Publications Contact

About Me

I combine GIS, remote sensing, machine learning, and spatial modeling to develop decision-ready geospatial products for environmental and climate applications. My work integrates LiDAR, SAR, optical satellite imagery, and large-scale computational workflows to quantify vegetation dynamics, land cover change, forest structure, wildfire-treatment outcomes, agricultural systems, and coastal ecosystem responses.

My research is motivated by the need to connect remote sensing science with real-world decision-making in climate adaptation, ecosystem monitoring, hazard risk assessment, and sustainability planning.

Selected Projects

High-Resolution Forest Canopy Height & Biomass Mapping

Developed annual 10 m canopy height, canopy cover, and aboveground biomass products using GEDI LiDAR and multi-sensor remote sensing for forest monitoring and wildfire-treatment outcome assessment.

Data: GEDI, Landsat, Sentinel, topography, climate variables

Methods: Multi-sensor fusion, machine learning, spatial validation, uncertainty analysis

Tools: Python, Google Earth Engine, XGBoost, ArcGIS Pro, HPC

Wildfire Risk and Transmission Line Exposure Mapping

Built geospatial workflows to evaluate wildfire and wind-related exposure along power transmission corridors, supporting infrastructure resilience and hazard risk decision-making.

Data: Fire risk layers, wind data, transmission lines, vegetation, terrain

Methods: Spatial overlay, risk modeling, exposure analysis, buffer-based summaries

Tools: Python, GeoPandas, ArcGIS Pro, raster processing

Automated Local Climate Zone Mapping

Produced a scalable framework for 25 m Local Climate Zone mapping using multi-source remote sensing, decision rules, and spatial machine learning across large urban extents.

Data: Remote sensing, land cover, building, terrain, and urban morphology variables

Methods: Decision rules, spatial ML, transfer learning, large-scale classification

Tools: Google Earth Engine, Python, HPC, machine learning

Rapid Rice Yield Estimation

Developed a machine learning pipeline for rice yield forecasting using integrated satellite imagery and meteorological data for agricultural monitoring in the Hindu-Kush Himalaya region.

Data: Satellite imagery, vegetation indices, weather, crop production data

Methods: Feature engineering, ML regression, operational monitoring

Tools: Python, remote sensing, Google Earth Engine, machine learning

Sundarbans Blue-Carbon Monitoring

Developed 10 m canopy height and biomass products for the Sundarbans mangrove forest to support tropical coastal ecosystem monitoring and blue-carbon assessment.

Data: GEDI LiDAR, Landsat, Sentinel, SAR, environmental covariates

Methods: Multi-sensor fusion, biomass modeling, temporal mapping

Tools: Python, Google Earth Engine, machine learning

Urban Heat, Tree Canopy, and Vulnerability Mapping

Integrated satellite-derived land surface temperature, tree canopy indicators, and social vulnerability data to identify neighborhoods with overlapping climate and environmental justice burdens.

Data: Landsat LST, ACS, CDC SVI, canopy data, urban indicators

Methods: Index development, spatial analysis, clustering, equity mapping

Tools: ArcGIS Pro, Python, Google Earth Engine
